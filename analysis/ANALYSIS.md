# Borrowing Readiness Assessment: Bad-Month Stress Simulator (GPT 5.5 XH)
**Date:** May 12, 2026

## Overview
The idea is good, but it is not completely new. The winning version is not just "a calculator"—it is a **bad-month survivability test for borrowing**.

### Verdict
Keep this concept and make it the strongest feature of the current borrowing readiness checker.

## Core Value Proposition
The idea is strong because it shifts the fundamental question for the borrower:

* **From:** *"Can I pay this loan in a normal month?"*
* **To:** *"Can I still pay if this month goes wrong?"*

This approach is emotionally concrete, easier to demonstrate, and more aligned with actual financial resilience. For sari-sari stores or small online sellers, this is a realistic necessity. Lower sales, delayed restocking, unpaid customer *utang*, or family emergencies can break repayment even when the loan initially looked affordable.

## Implementation & Risk
**The Risk:** If presented poorly, it becomes just another loan calculator with sliders. 

**The Solution:** The demo must follow a clear narrative:
1.  **Normal Month:** The loan looks manageable.
2.  **Bad Month:** Sales drop, an emergency expense appears, or restock costs rise.
3.  **Result:** Cash buffer goes negative before the repayment date.
4.  **App Suggestion:** Borrow less, choose a longer term, delay borrowing, or maintain a higher minimum buffer.

This is much more actionable and "judgeable" than generic financial literacy advice.

## Market Landscape
### Does this exist?
Adjacent versions exist, but there is no prominent Philippine product for informal sellers doing this exact thing.

### Existing Adjacent Tools
* **Loan Stress Calculators (Global):** [Calcix](https://calcix.net/calculators/loan-mortgage/loan-stress-scenario-calculator) offers stress scenarios (income drops, interest changes) for general consumers, but not specific to sari-sari store cash cycles.
* **Debt-Service Stress Calculators:** [Calcix](https://calcix.net/calculators/loan-mortgage/debt-service-stress-calculator) tests if income covers debt service in bad conditions, usually for business or real estate.
* **Cash-Flow Scenario Planning:** Tools like [Xero](https://www.xero.com/us/accounting-software/analytics/cash-flow/) and [Float](https://intercom.help/floatapp/en/articles/1719419-scenario-planning) allow "what-if" modeling but require formal accounting data.
* **Repayment Stress Advice:** [Funding Guru](https://fundingguru.com/blog/start-up-loan-calculators-what-the-monthly-cost-really-tells-you) recommends modeling downside cases like seasonality or illness.
* **PH Loan Marketplace/Calculators:** [Savvy Spender](https://savvy-spender.vercel.app/) and [Digido](https://digido.com.ph/) offer general tools but lack the specific "bad month survivability" focus.
* **Sari-Sari Management Apps:** [Sari.PH](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.packworks.microstoreapp) handles inventory and bookkeeping but does not center on loan stress simulation.

## Critical Assessment
While stress testing standard financial methodology, it is hackathon-original if localized sharply:

* **Target:** Sari-sari store owners and small online sellers.
* **Timing:** Immediately before taking a loan.
* **Stress Cases:** Low sales weeks, supply delays, family emergencies, unpaid customer *utang*.
* **Output:** "You survive" vs. "Repayment breaks cash flow."
* **Recommendations:** Safer amounts, adjusted due dates, or required buffers.

### Final Pitch Framing
> "A borrowing readiness checker with a bad-month stress simulator for informal Filipino sellers."

**Strategy:** Do not pitch it as a standalone simulator. Pitch it as the defining feature that makes your borrowing checker superior to standard calculators.
